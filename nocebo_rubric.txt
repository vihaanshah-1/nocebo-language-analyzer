
Category descriptions:

1. threat_intensity: Sentences containing words suggesting high severity or fatal outcomes (e.g., 'death', 'stroke', 'anaphylaxis', 'serious complications').
2. uncertainty: Phrases implying unpredictability or unknown outcomes (e.g., 'possible', 'rarely', 'may').
3. vivid_side_effects: Terms describing unpleasant side effects in vivid detail (e.g., 'nausea', 'vomiting', 'pain', 'itchy rash').
4. directive_harshness: Imperative or restrictive language instructing patients to comply or avoid certain actions (e.g., 'must not', 'do not', 'stop taking').

Label assignment:
0 = neutral
1 = mild anxiety trigger (one category)
2 = moderate (two categories)
3 = high (three or more categories)
